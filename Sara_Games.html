Sara Games

 “Unconventional Beauty, to me, is about changing your viewpoint on how you see the world around you. It is about taking something mundane and turning it into something to be in awe of..."

In a well-lit studio in the outskirts of Raleigh, Sara organizes the wardrobe we’d carefully selected for this shoot on a clothing rack. Gazing at the soft, subtle shades of cream and white, her hands work nimbly hanging up the garments as she answers my questions.

Q: “What is unconventional beauty, and what does it mean to you?”

The simplistic, all-white location of our shoot seems to mirror the direct nature with which she answers my question… as if she knew what I was about to ask
before I did.

A: “Unconventional Beauty, to me, is about changing your viewpoint on how you see the world around you. It is about taking something mundane and turning it into something to be in awe of. So often we see ourselves in our mind’s eye as an oversimplified, plain, daily idea of who we are. Unconventional beauty is taking our “plain” and transforming our perception of it into something to admire; and to love it wholeheartedly.”

Now finished organizing the clothing on their hangers, Sara then takes a seat in the middle of the studio on a plain wooden stool. She looks at ease in front of the camera, and the combination of the ambient lighting and the freshly applied baby oil on her skin lights up her entire complexion and makes her appear as an otherworldly, ethereal being. Simply put, she is breathtaking.

Q: How are some ways you’ve recognized the beauty in an otherwise “ugly” situation?

Perched on her stool, she looks confident and self-assured as she ponders the answer to my question.

A: “Choosing to see growth in ugly situations is when the beauty begins to unfold. Feeling your worst and being at your lowest can create a sense of beauty knowing that you can only grow and become greater from this moment on! Beauty is in everything if we only choose to see it.”

To Sara, unconventional beauty isn’t exclusively related to physical appearance. Instead, it’s about the way we carry ourselves in a society that praises the highlight reels of our respective existences. Rather than only looking at the best situations as an ideal, Sara knows that the most beautiful way of being is to look at the plain, every day moments and realize the beauty within. Just as she said, “beauty is in everything, if we only choose to see it”. May we all adopt her mentality and strive to see the beauty in between the traditionally glorious moments that life has to offer, and look for that which is Unconventionally Beautiful.
